Neeraj Chopra

Neeraj Chopra is a highly accomplished and celebrated Indian athlete, particularly known for his excellence in javelin throw.


Here are some key points to include in a tribute page for Neeraj Chopra:
  • Early Life: Neeraj Chopra was born on December 24, 1997, in Khandra, Panipat, Haryana, India. His interest in sports, particularly athletics, was evident from a young age.
  • Javelin Throw Career: Neeraj gained prominence in the field of javelin throw, showcasing exceptional talent and dedication.
    He broke onto the international scene by winning the gold medal at the 2016 IAAF World U20 Championships in Bydgoszcz, Poland.
  • Record-Breaking Achievements: Neeraj Chopra holds the national record for the longest javelin throw by an Indian athlete. He set a new Indian record with a throw of 88.07 meters at the 2018 Asian Games in Jakarta, Indonesia.
  • Olympic Glory: Neeraj Chopra made history at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics by winning the gold medal in the men's javelin throw. His winning throw of 87.58 meters not only secured India's first-ever gold in athletics but also made him the second individual gold medalist for India in Olympic history
